## Origin Story

The C-momo — short for "crispy momo" or sometimes credited to a Kathmandu shop whose owner's name began with "C" — emerged in the early 2010s as Kathmandu's most innovative momo variant. Unlike closed dumplings, the C-momo leaves the filling partially exposed at the top and is steamed on a special perforated plate, then finished with a drizzle of spiced butter or oil.

The open top allows direct steam contact with the filling, creating a slightly caramelised, intensely flavourful surface that closed momos can't achieve. It was an instant sensation, spawning imitations across Nepal within months.

## History & Cultural Significance

C-momos represent the modern era of momo innovation in Nepal. While traditional momos hadn't changed much in centuries, the 2010s brought an explosion of creativity: jhol momo, chocolate momo, cheese momo, and the C-momo. Food bloggers and Instagram drove their popularity, turning specific C-momo shops into pilgrimage sites for foodies.

The *"C-momo trail"* — visiting Kathmandu's top C-momo shops in a single afternoon — has become a tourist activity recommended by travel blogs and guidebooks.

> **Fun Fact:** The original C-momo shop in Kathmandu reportedly uses a secret spice blend containing 14 ingredients, and the recipe is known only to the owner and his wife.

## What Makes It Special

- **Open Top:** The exposed filling creates a Maillard reaction during steaming/finishing, adding umami depth.
- **Sauce Integration:** A spiced oil or butter sauce pools in the open top, basting the filling from above.
- **Visual Appeal:** The open design makes C-momos highly photogenic — they dominate Nepali food Instagram.
- **Texture Layers:** Soft dough base, juicy filling centre, and slightly crispy caramelised top.

## Filling & Sauce

| Ingredient (Filling) | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Minced buff or chicken | 400 g |
| Onion, finely diced | 1 large |
| Cilantro | ¼ cup |
| Garlic-ginger paste | 1 tbsp |
| Cumin, coriander powder | ½ tsp each |
| Soy sauce | 1 tsp |
| Salt & pepper | to taste |

| Ingredient (C-Momo Sauce) | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Butter | 2 tbsp |
| Garlic, minced | 2 cloves |
| Chilli flakes | 1 tsp |
| Szechuan pepper, ground | ¼ tsp |
| Spring onion, chopped | 2 tbsp |

## Preparation

1. Prepare dough as usual. Roll into circles.
2. Place filling in centre. Gather dough edges upward, leaving the top open (like a cup).
3. Steam for 12–15 minutes.
4. Meanwhile, melt butter with garlic, chilli flakes, and *timur*. Sizzle until fragrant.
5. Drizzle the spiced butter over the open tops of the steamed momos.
6. Garnish with spring onion and serve immediately.

## Interesting Tidbits

- C-momo competitions have sprung up in Kathmandu, where chefs compete on filling flavour, sauce creativity, and plating.
- Some shops now offer "C-jhol momo" — an open C-momo sitting in jhol broth — combining two innovations.
- The C-momo concept has been exported to momo shops in London, New York, and Sydney by Nepali diaspora entrepreneurs.
