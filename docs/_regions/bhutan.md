## Overview

Bhutan shares deep cultural ties with Tibet, and momos have been part of Bhutanese cuisine for centuries. However, Bhutanese momos have a distinctive twist: they often incorporate the country's beloved *ema datshi* (chilli-cheese) flavours, featuring local soft cheese and fiery Bhutanese chillies inside the dumpling.

## The Bhutanese Style

| Feature | Bhutanese Style |
|---|---|
| **Signature filling** | Cheese and chilli (*ema datshi* momo) |
| **Heat level** | Extremely spicy — Bhutan uses chilli as a vegetable, not a condiment |
| **Dough** | Standard wheat flour, similar to Tibetan style |
| **Common proteins** | Pork, beef, yak, and cheese (vegetarian) |
| **Serving** | Often with *ezay* (Bhutanese chilli sauce) |

## Cultural Significance

- Momos are served during *tshechu* (religious festivals) and family celebrations.
- Bhutan's national dish is *ema datshi* (chilli and cheese stew) — the momo version wraps this national flavour inside dough.
- Communal momo-making is a winter tradition, especially in rural eastern Bhutan.
