### Background

Jhol achar is the liquid chutney that birthed the jhol momo phenomenon. Thin enough to be a soup, intensely flavoured enough to be a sauce — it bridges the gap between condiment and course. Every momo shop in Nepal has its own closely guarded jhol recipe.

### Ingredients

- 4 medium tomatoes, roughly chopped
- 3 tbsp sesame seeds, dry-roasted
- 4 dried red chillies
- 1 tsp Szechuan pepper (*timur*)
- ¼ tsp fenugreek seeds
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 2 cups water
- 1 tbsp mustard oil
- Salt, sugar, and lemon juice to taste
- Fresh cilantro for garnish

### Directions

1. Dry-roast sesame seeds, *timur*, fenugreek, and dried chillies until fragrant.
2. Blend roasted spices with tomatoes, garlic, and turmeric into a smooth paste.
3. Heat mustard oil in a pan. Pour in the paste and cook for 3 minutes, stirring.
4. Add water and bring to a gentle simmer. Cook for 10 minutes.
5. Season with salt, a pinch of sugar, and lemon juice.
6. The consistency should be broth-like — add more water if too thick.
7. Ladle into bowls over steamed momos, or serve alongside as a dipping broth.
8. Garnish with fresh cilantro.

### Tips

- Mustard oil adds authentic Nepali pungency — if unavailable, use sesame oil.
- Strain through a sieve for a smoother broth, or leave chunky for rustic style.
