### Background

This chutney draws on the shared Tibetan-Chinese heritage of the momo. Szechuan pepper (*timur* in Nepali) and dried red chillies are bloomed in hot oil, creating a numbing-spicy condiment that pairs brilliantly with crispy fried and kothey momos.

### Ingredients

- ¼ cup neutral oil (or sesame oil)
- 3 tbsp dried red chilli flakes
- 1 tsp Szechuan pepper (*timur*), crushed
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp ginger, grated
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- ½ tsp sugar
- Pinch of salt

### Directions

1. Heat oil until shimmering but not smoking.
2. Remove from heat. Immediately add chilli flakes, crushed *timur*, garlic, and ginger (careful — it sizzles!).
3. Stir gently and let the residual heat bloom the spices (30 seconds).
4. Add soy sauce, rice vinegar, and sugar. Mix well.
5. Let cool to room temperature. The flavour deepens as it sits.
6. Serve in a small bowl alongside fried or kothey momos.

### Tips

- Make a big batch — this chutney keeps for weeks in a sealed jar in the fridge.
- Add toasted sesame seeds for extra nuttiness.
- For a milder version, reduce chilli flakes and increase Szechuan pepper for a numbing (rather than burning) sensation.
