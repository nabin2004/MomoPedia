### Background

No momo is complete without *achar* (chutney). The tomato-sesame version is the gold standard across Nepal — found at every momo stall from Kathmandu to Pokhara. Its smoky, tangy, nutty flavour profile complements every momo variety.

### Ingredients

- 4 medium tomatoes, roasted or charred
- 2 tbsp sesame seeds, dry-roasted
- 3 dried red chillies (adjust to taste)
- 3 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp Szechuan pepper (*timur*), dry-roasted
- ½ tsp fenugreek seeds, dry-roasted
- 1 tbsp cilantro, chopped
- Salt to taste
- 1 tsp lemon juice
- 2 tbsp water (as needed)

### Directions

1. Char tomatoes directly over a flame or under a broiler until blackened and soft.
2. Dry-roast sesame seeds, dried chillies, *timur*, and fenugreek in a pan until fragrant (2 minutes).
3. Grind roasted spices in a mortar or blender to a coarse paste.
4. Add charred tomatoes and garlic; blend to a chunky consistency.
5. Season with salt and lemon juice. Add water to reach desired consistency.
6. Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve at room temperature.

### Tips

- For a smokier flavour, char the tomatoes directly on a gas flame.
- This chutney keeps well in the fridge for up to 5 days.
- Adjust *timur* quantity — it creates a distinctive numbing sensation.
